ANAEMIA

“The truth about what happens to us in the world keeps changing...it never stops.”








ABOUT:

  • The condition of having a lower-than-normal number of red blood cells or quantity of hemoglobin.
  • Normal results vary, but in general are:
    1. Male: 13.8 to 17.2 gm/dL
    2. Female: 12.1 to 15.1 gm/dL(Note: gm/dL = grams per deciliter)
  • Anaemia has three main causes: blood loss, lack of red blood cell production, and high rates of red blood cell destruction.
  • Conditions that may lead to anaemia include
    1. Heavy periods
    2. Pregnancy
    3. Ulcers
    4. Colon polyps or colon cancer
    5. Inherited disorders
    6. A diet that does not have enough iron, folic acid or vitamin B12
    7. Blood disorders such as sickle cell anaemia and thalassemia, or cancer
    8. Aplastic anaemia, a condition that can be inherited or acquired
    9. Anaemia can make you feel tired, cold, dizzy, and irritable. You may be short of breath or have a headache.

SYMPTOMS:

  • The most common symptom of anaemia is fatigue or weakness.
  • Other signs and symptoms of anaemia include:
    1. Shortness of breath
    2. Dizziness
    3. Headache
    4. Coldness in the hands and feet
    5. Pale skin
    6. Chest pain

TREATMENT:

  • Iron supplements: The most commonly prescribed supplement is ferrous sulphate, taken orally (by mouth) two or three times a day.
  • Dietary supplements: Iron-rich foods include:
    1. Dark-green leafy vegetables, such as spinach
    2. Iron-fortified cereals
    3. Whole grains, such as brown rice
    4. Beans
    5. Nuts
    6. Meat
    7. Apricots
