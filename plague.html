PLAGUE

“Prevention is better than cure and it all depends on us which one we want to choose.”








ABOUT:

  • Plague is a communicable disease, caused by bacterium Yersinia pestis
  • Transmitted between vertebrate animals and humans
  • Non-motile, non-acid fast, non spore forming, gram-negative cocco-bacillus. Y. pestis is easily destroyed by sunlight, high temperatures, and disinfectants.
  • As with many primarily zoonotic diseases, plague is a very severe disease in people, with case fatality rates of 50-60% if left untreated.

SYMPTOMS:

  • Incubation period (time taken between catching an infection and appearance of symptoms) of the disease is 3-7 days.
    1. fever
    2. chills
    3. head and body-aches and weakness
    4. vomiting and nausea.

TREATMENT:

  • Precautions to be taken against the flea bites
  • Not to handle animal carcasses in plague-endemic areas
  • Avoid direct contact with infected tissues such as suppurating buboes
  • Avoid exposure to patients with pneumonic plague
  • Various socio-epidemiological factors responsible for an outbreak in that particular area.
