GOITRE

“The goitre of the world,swells up with the desire for conquest and bursts with achievements which everyday brings. A week has room for the boldest climax of the human drive for expansion.”








ABOUT:

  • The term “goitre” simply refers to the abnormal enlargement of the thyroid gland.
  • The thyroid gland is a small butterfly-shaped gland in the neck, just in front of the windpipe (trachea).
  • It produces thyroid hormones, which helps to regulate the body's metabolism (the process that turns food into energy).
  • A swelling of thyroid gland can further lead to a swelling of the neck or larynx (voice box).
  • Goitre is also known as Iodine deficiency disorder as Iodine deficiency is the most common cause of prevalence of goitre.
  • It is estimated that more than 1.5 billion people in the world are at risk from iodine deficiency disorders out of which about 200 million people reside in India.
  • Types of goitre:There are two different types of goitre:
    1. Diffuse small goitre, where entire thyroid gland enlarges to a larger size and feels smooth to the touch
    2. Nodular goitre, where certain sections, or "nodules", of thyroid gland enlarge and feel lumpy to the touch

SYMPTOMS:

  • Hyperthyroidism:
    1. Weight loss
    2. Increased appetite
    3. Heat intolerance.
  • Hypothyroidism:
    1. Weight gain
    2. Constipation
    3. Cold intolerance
    4. Fatigue
    5. Dry and scaly skin

TREATMENT:

  • hormone therapy.
  • Thionamide drugs
  • Radioactive Iodine
  • Iodine supplements
  • Surgery.
